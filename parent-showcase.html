What Is CIRCUIT?

CIRCUIT is a browser game where two players compete on one shared map. The goal is not just to own many states, but to connect them into the largest single cluster.

1 Control territory

Players move across neighboring states and buy only the public targets currently available.

2 Manage a budget

Everyone starts with 15 coins. Holding earns money, but coins cap at 20, so timing matters.

3 Build connections

Scoring rewards the largest connected cluster, which makes bridges and blockades powerful.

4 Adapt to surprises

Secret contracts and stealing add hidden goals, risk, and late-game comebacks.

How It Plays

Each turn is simple, but the choices create strategy: move now, save coins, claim a target, or attack a key connection.

Public Flop targets are marked with F. A secret contract is private, shown here as ? only for explanation.
Turn choice
Roll or Hold

Roll a d3 to move 1 to 3 steps, or Hold to gain 5 coins and end the turn.

Movement
Move across borders

Own and empty states are free to cross. Opponent states cost 1 coin per step.

Claim
Buy a public or secret target

Only 3 public Flop states are claimable at once. Secret contracts are private targets that reward memory and planning.

Attack
Steal a key state

Pay double a state price to seize it from the opponent and possibly split their connected cluster.

Win
Score the biggest cluster

After the final turn, the largest connected group wins. Ties use total states owned, then coins spent buying states.

What I Built

The project grew from a local two-player game into a fuller product with AI, online multiplayer, saving, replays, and rule verification.

Complete browser game

The main game runs from one HTML file, with the board, rules, turn flow, scoring, and UI all included.

AI opponents

Player 2 can be human, random, heuristic AI, or an optional OpenAI-powered bot through a local server.

Online multiplayer

A Node.js and Socket.IO server lets two people play by creating and joining a short room code.

Save files

Games can be saved and loaded as JSON, keeping progress controlled by the player instead of hidden browser storage.

Replay viewer

Finished games can be downloaded and watched turn by turn, which helps explain decisions after a match.

Tested rules

Simulated games checked rule invariants like currency limits, legal movement, Flop refills, and AI legality.
