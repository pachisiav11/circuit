CIRCUIT

Two players, one fixed 25-state map, 15 currency each. Build the largest connected cluster over 20 turns.

Each turn, Roll a d3 then move your token that many steps — one adjacent state at a time, you pick the route. You lose 1 currency for every opponent tile you pass through or stop on. Move onto an unclaimed Flop state to claim it, which ends your turn. Or Hold to bank +5 currency and end your turn without moving.

Hot-seat: two humans share this screen. Pass the device each turn.

How to Play

🎯 Goal. Over 20 turns, build the biggest single connected cluster of states. You can own several separate groups, but only your largest connected cluster counts as your score. Ties are broken by: most total states owned, then highest total spent buying states.

💰 Money. You start with 15 coins and the only way to gain more is Holding (+5). Coins are capped at 20, so you can't sit and hoard. Spend wisely: every coin is precious.

🎲 Each turn, pick ONE:

① Roll the d3 → move your token 1–3 steps across borders. You choose the route, one tile at a time. When you click an affordable Flop tile, a popup asks whether to Claim it (ends your turn) or just Pass through — you're never forced to buy a tile you only walk over. The board is several moves wide, so getting somewhere takes planning.
② Hold → skip moving and bank +5 coins. Your turn ends. This is how a broke player gets back in the game.

🚶 Moving costs. Stepping across your own land or empty land is free. Stepping onto an opponent's tile costs 1 coin each. You can't make a move you can't pay for — if you're out of coins, you can't enter enemy tiles.

🟩 The colours while moving:

Blue tile = a free step you can take · orange tile = opponent tile, costs 1 to step on · bright green = click to claim or pass through · dark green = a Flop state you can afford to reach this turn · yellow = a Flop state in reach but too expensive to claim right now (walk toward it / save up).

🗡️ Stealing. You can seize an opponent's state instead of claiming a Flop tile. Step onto their tile (the usual −1 toll), then pay double its price to take it — this ends your turn (shown in gold on the board). It's an expensive, all-in play, but taking a key "bridge" tile can split your opponent's cluster in two. A tile that just changed hands can't be stolen back on the very next turn.

🎯 Secret Contract. You always hold one private target state, shown only to you. To claim it: walk your token onto it (it's unmarked — remember it by name), then in your panel press Reveal → Show → Buy (same price as a Flop tile). Only that deliberate purchase reveals it — walking over it does nothing and exposes nothing. It's pulled from the shared pool (never in the Flop; the opponent can't claim or steal it), and buying draws a new hidden one.

🃏 The Flop. Only the 3 states in the Flop are claimable. When you claim one, it's replaced by a fresh state. The price tiers run from cheap $2 edges up to the pricey $8 centre (GRAIL) — central states connect to more neighbours.

🏆 Winning. After turn 10, whoever has the most states in a single unbroken cluster wins. If that's tied, the player who owns more states total wins; if still tied, the one who spent more coins buying states wins. If everything is still tied, it's a draw.
